P126 AOI Circuit

DE - Ms. Chou - Period 6

Project Dates: 10/20/2025 - 10/31/2025

Full Project Documentation ↗

Our project aimed to develop a smart traffic light control system using AOI logic gates to manage flow at a T-intersection. We designed and built a prototype on classroom breadboards with switches as sensors, LEDs as signals, and integrated circuits to handle vehicle detection and light changes. The system followed guidelines: 8 inputs for lane occupancy and right-of-way denials, 4 outputs for light signals, completed in 2 weeks while complying with basic safety rules like no conflicting greens.

While I wasn’t the project manager, I actively contributed by deriving my output's logic expression and truth table, simplifying it using Boolean laws, and wiring my section of the breadboard. I also troubleshot integrations and ensured team consensus on the final Multisim simulation.

Working with breadboards and basic ICs taught me to optimize simple hardware for reliable logic. Though not industrial-grade, we adapted them as our only option. This mirrors real-world urban traffic systems where budgets force improvisation with available tech to reduce congestion and accidents.

Our circuit had faulty outputs initially due to misconnected gates, disrupting signal logic. We debugged by checking connections and remaking wires with color-coding, achieving correct LED behaviors after iterations.

Sensors (switches) also failed to simulate consistent detection at first, due to unplugged ICs. We tested individually and reorganized wiring until stable. Throughout, I demonstrated problem-solving by addressing logic errors and hardware issues, contributing to our team's success.
